  [1X1.1 [33X[0;0YGeneral aims[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe [5XGAP[105X package [5Xglabella[105X provides a low level interface to software that can
  compute  automorphisms, isomorphisms and canonical labellings of graphs. The
  graphs  can  be  directed  or  undirected,  and  a  vertex  colouring can be
  specified. Duplicate edges between vertices are ignored.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThese  interfaces  do  not intend to be feature rich and user friendly. They
  are  targeted to experienced GAP users and package authors, who can use them
  for  their  specific  classes  of  graphs  and  other  discrete mathematical
  structures, without the neccessity of compiling C and C++ codes.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  more  specialized  algorithms  and  methods of the theory of graphs, we
  recommend the packages [5XDigraphs[105X [DBJM+19] and [5XGRAPE[105X [Soi19].[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  the  present  version  of  the  package, we have access to the following
  solvers:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ybliss:   A  Tool  for  Computing  Automorphism  Groups  and  Canonical
        Labellings          of          Graphs          (version         0.73)
        ([7Xhttp://www.tcs.hut.fi/Software/bliss/[107X)  written by Tommi Junttila and
        Petteri Kaski [JK07].[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Ynauty  and  Traces:  Graph  Canonical Labelling and Automorphism Group
        Computation (version 2.7R1) ([7Xhttps://pallini.di.uniroma1.it/[107X), written
        by Brendan McKay and Adolfo Piperno [MP14].[133X
  
  
  [1X1.2 [33X[0;0YInstallation[133X[101X

        [11Xpkg[111X directory.[133X

        [4X[32X[104X
